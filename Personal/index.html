Story's if you are curious

Career change

I always wanted to be a welder. I spent my highschool career taking shop classes and information extracuriculars about the trade. I had a half day ctc program where I welded for 4 hours a day. The work was diffucult to say the least. 3500+ degree flames and 100+ degree heat every day. However I enjoyed my work creating grills, tables,small building frames and even motercylces. But What made me reconsider my welding career was the enviorment not the work. Then, my junior year I decieded I wanted a change of scenery so I got into web development. Ever since I discovered web dev I have been on a mission to improve my skills and change rapidly.

Business?

Ever since I was very young I had a ambition to sell and create. I made a binder I called it my Idea binder. I planned trips, inventions, collaberation techniques and small businesses. For example, I started a cookie business in school, I "hired" a couple of my friends to sell all types of cookies that I baked from stratch. It was quite successful, we made about 300 a month. When you are in middle school thats a lot of money. We even expanded to candy and drinks. However we were stoped by the principle due to we were not allowed to sell unless is was through the school. I never stopped trying recycleing businesses, yard sale resale, and 3D printed wire holders. I am not stopping now either.

Hard work is key

What I have noticed in my short life time is you will not move up in society unless you work hard. That may seem obvious but realistly many spend thier days unproductivly and expect results or complain about the results they recieve. I have no problem if you are comfortable where you are but for those who have a dream of their success but dont but in the work, they are lying to themselves. Continuing the same behaviour over and over but expecting a different result is called insanity. Work,achive, fail,succed this is the process.

Busy

The world is always moving with or without you. As a society we embrase information at ever turn. Social media, radio, television web surfing, and much more. We are always occupied, always on the move but is it healthy? Thats a question for yourself to answer truley but I think not. There is no problem with the sharing of information however being occupied with every possible task may be to much for some to handle. What do we do about it? The internet and the vertial world is not going away if anything it will be more involed later down the road. However, it is an indivdual choice to "unplug" from time to time. Take a walk, play a sport, even just put your electronics down and read a book. Who knows you may enjoy a the break?

Appreciation

We learn durning our lives to take the moment in and savor the good times. As children we observe and absorb the world around us. As teenagers we learn the ropes and want to experiment without a care in the world. The ansty teen who want to be alone but does not fully understand the magnitude of true responsiblity and acts like they do. Young adults, finally independent but always confused about their future. Then, the company. After the slow learning process of responsibl ity (which never ends), we take on new leaps of our life like a family, stable job and new adventures. Then, the routine of life settles in we get in the flow of work, family, relax and repeat. It may sound daunting but its quite the opposite. During every phase we learn and we live. Life is mysterious but never get discouraged becuase their is always a future, their is always reason for apperciation.
